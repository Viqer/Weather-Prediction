Welcome to Cyclone Prediction System

This platform leverages deep learning models like CNNs to predict the occurrence and severity of cyclones. It utilizes satellite imagery and other real-time data sources to make accurate forecasts.

About the Project

This project aims to predict cyclonic conditions using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN). Our approach involves training models on datasets like INSAT3D Infrared & Raw Cyclone Imagery (2012-2021) and evaluating their accuracy and efficiency to find the best solution.

Datasets Used

  • INSAT3D Infrared & Raw Cyclone Imagery: 2012-2021 dataset covering cyclone formations and intensities.
  • Real-Time Cyclone Footage: Data from NOAA, IMD, and other sources.
  • Global Weather Data: Supplementary data on temperature, wind speed, and air pressure.

Model Training & Evaluation

We are experimenting with the following CNN models:

  • VGG-16 / VGG-19: For feature extraction from satellite images.
  • ResNet-50: Deeper networks for capturing complex patterns.
  • EfficientNet: Optimized for balancing accuracy and speed.

The best model will be selected based on accuracy, inference speed, and resource consumption.
